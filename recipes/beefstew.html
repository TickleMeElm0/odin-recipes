Colombia beef Stew (Sudado de Res)

Sudado de Carne is a traditional Colombian beef stew. This dish is the perfect one pot meal served over white rice, a light salad and avocado on the side. It's a stew made with beef, chicken, pork, seafood or meatballs simmered in a flavorful broth with potatoes, yuca, spices, fresh herbs and vegetables like bell pepper, carrots, garlic and onions. There are many variations of this dish in Colombia and different recipes depending in the region and the cook.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef for stew (chuck)
  • 1 tablespoon oil canola or vegetable
  • ½ cup chopped white or yellow onion
  • 2 scallions, chopped
  • ½ cup chopped red bell pepper
  • 2 minced garlic cloves
  • 2 cups chopped tomato
  • ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • ½ teaspoon sazón with color
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon pepper
  • 3 medium potatoes peeled and cut in half
  • ½ pound frozen or fresh yuca cassava
  • 3 cups beef broth

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the onion, scallions and red pepper and sauté until onions are translucent, about 3 minutes. Then add the tomatoes, garlic, salt and ground pepper and sauté for 5 more minutes.
  2. Add the beef, broth, sazón and cumin powder. Reduce the heat to medium low cover and cook for 45 to 50 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the potatoes, yuca and cilantro and cook for an additional 30 minutes or until the vegetables are tender. Add more broth or water if the stew is getting to dry.
  4. Serve over white rice.
